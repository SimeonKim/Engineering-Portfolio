    About Me

    Philosophy &
    Who I Am

    Y'30
    Yale University — Class of 2030
    Incoming freshman. Looking to continue building at the intersection of mechanical engineering and real-world impact.

    Why I Build

    My father is a pastor and my mother is a social worker. Growing up my family used to host several families every week where I had the privilege of learning about the life and the thoughts of toddlers to great grandparents, people who didn't graduate high school and Oxford educated historians, people who just came to this country and people who have never left. I got to know people. That taught me to pay attention. Not just to the problem on the surface, but to the person carrying it.

    I used to think engineering was about being skilled. Then at Robard Bionics, I watched an amputee talk about how his prosthetic made him feel more alive — and I realized I'd been asking the wrong question the whole time. It was never about how good I was. It was about what I was building, and for who.

    How I Think

    When something's broken or unclear, I start by taking it apart — what are the pieces, where are the constraints, what's most likely to fail. A lot of that happens in my head before anything else. I'll rotate through different angles, run scenarios, test ideas until something clicks. It's how I've always worked, and I've learned to trust it.

    I've also come to realize that not every interesting problem is a technical one. Some of the messiest systems I've dealt with involve people — how they communicate, whether they actually trust each other, who owns what when things go wrong. I try to bring the same care to those.

    What I'm Looking For

    I'm looking for an environment where I can do serious work — where I'm expected to think, contribute, and take ownership.

    I want to design systems that are both functional and thoughtful. I want to be in rooms where ideas are tested, challenged, and improved. And I'm looking for opportunities where I can make a measurable impact.

    Give me a chance, and I will make it worth your investment.

    Want to talk? I'm always up for a good engineering conversation.
